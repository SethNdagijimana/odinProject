Potato Biscuits Recipe

    INGREDIENTS

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons baking powder
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup butter or margarine
  • 1 ½ cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • ⅓ cup cold water
  • ⅓ cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons milk, or as needed

    Step 1

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

    2. Step 2

  2. In a medium bowl, stir together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Cut in butter, until pieces of butter are no larger than peas. Use a fork to stir in mashed potatoes, breaking them up into chunks. Make a well in the mixture, and pour in egg, water and milk. Stir into a loose dough using the fork.

    3. Step 3

  3. Turn dough out onto a floured surface, and knead for 6 or 8 times so the dough holds together. Pat the dough out to about 3/4 inch thickness, and cut into circles with a biscuit cutter or drinking glass. Place biscuits onto the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops with 2 tablespoons milk.

    4. Step 4

  4. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes in the preheated oven, or until bottoms are golden.
